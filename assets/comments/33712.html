Johnny Cash joined him on a duet, singing “Girl of the North Country” and it isn’t Dylan who ruins the harmonies, but Cash.His last – to my taste – “classic” album isDesire. I first heard ‘Black Diamond Bay’, the tale of a woman, a volcano, and a panama hat, when I was sixteen. True, that album included the regrettable ‘Hurricane’ which had a lot of FM airplay at the time, bemoaning the “racist” murder conviction of “Hurricane” Carter.* Pity, because it is one hell of a song, with its driving strings, tight drumming and righteous (sounding) anger. Take the same song and make it about the Brunswick 3, and you’ve got yourself a dissidentpiece de resistance.My favorite all-time Dylan recording (except for a few others, if you know what I mean) is ‘Sarah’, also onDesire. An emotional masterpiece which, I understand, is about his wife and their failing relationship. Comparable in every way to Gordon Lightfoot’s “If You Could Read My Mind” as a song of despair over a failed marriage. A beautiful, thoughtful, masterful recording. You’ll never hear it on the radio and I doubt he ever performed it in concert these last decades, but it is among his very best.Finally,Bringing All Back Home, from 1965, what with ‘Gates of Eden’, “It’s All Over Now, Baby Blue” and the incredible “It’s Alright Ma”, is a personal favorite.
