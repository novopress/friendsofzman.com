The phrase “separation of powers,” as well as the political doctrine, originates among Puritan political theorists during the English Civil War. It doesn’t exist before that. The political theory that it displaced was called mixed government, which was the belief that the “King in Parliament” was a unitary system of government. The separation of powers was then taken up as the definition of constitutional government by Montesquieu who was the primary influence on the Founders. But the separation of powers doctrine, as the core structure of the American Constitution, originates with the English Puritans.
