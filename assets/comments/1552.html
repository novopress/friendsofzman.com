So that’s half a milly for Katie and Billie.Imagine however, that you knew Katie and Billie would always have enough to eat, would always be fully-clothed and sheltered and would be working from the age of 12 or 13. This is where the return to agriculture comes in.People only need work 25-30 hours a week and modern farming isn’t exactly the back-breaking drudgery it used to be. Cottage industries like clothing, shoe making, pottery and wood turning provide for the tangible goods we need. The advances in aqua/hydroponics along with solar power means a big backyard could supply what a medium sized farm previously could with about one-tenth of the resources.It may sound a bit Pol Pot but it really could work. No more soul-draining commute, staring at a computer for hours on end, worrying about the bitch from human-resources deciding your family’s fortunes or losing 50 hours of your week shuffling papers for people who don’t care if you live or die.If you offered jobs for life for people to pick corn and cucumbers 25 hours a week, I think you would get a line around the block.
