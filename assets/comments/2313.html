Central planning fails because it is all about allocation, not efficiency. A free market is always adjusting allocations, features, quality, and price charged on the good or service, in an attempt to zero in on the moving target of what combination of costs and features is the most desired, at that particular moment. Central planning decides on features, stacks up the inventory, and then distributes it out. It has no feedback loop to make things more appropriate for the marketplace at any given moment.
