Z,I think where the story of the robotic future gets into the realm of hype and fantasy is typically the story jumps immediately to some end point of AI and robots working in harmony to make our daily lives better or worse depending on which future state you subscribe to. The reality is that the path between here and there is going to be fraught with twists and turns that no one can correctly predict and itself subject to disruptive innovations. The key point here is that on in incremental scale, the benefits of machine based automation, or tasks being executed by autonomous agents is has started to become available to the regular consumer versus purely available to the industrial consumer with deep pockets. So from robotic welders on car assembly lines to applications like google maps or waze that can optimize your travel time and route in real time, we are starting to see the benefits of early robotic technology. It is not encased in a humanoid form and perhaps that will never be the end result or be limited to certain applications (witness the advances in prosthetics in medicine is a great example of primitive robotics at work). Perhaps the future of robotics is a world full of intelligent apps that take some of the drudgery out of re-planning in the event of something changing or incorporating more precise forecasts to help reduce congestion or delays to any activity prone to it.At the end of the day it is a set of technologies, a toolbox, that requires a human to figure out where to apply it in scale or to achieve a scalable outcome. The creators of the toolbox can have their own fantasies of what they expect it to be, but that does not preclude someone else re-purposing it. To the comment above regarding robotic sex devices, I would not rule this out as an accelerant. Afterall, it was pornography that mainstreamed the VHS standard, streaming video on the internet and the earliest adopter of internet payments.
