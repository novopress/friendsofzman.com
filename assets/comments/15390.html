Think of how home values are now followed on Zillow. Pixels on a screen, calculated from an algorithm. That is how your home is “valued”. This comes out of financial instruments trading minute-to-minute for their own relative value, not for any real world need. The real world need sets up some sort of intinsic underlying value to the instrument (what it actually costs to purchase land and build a house, for example), but financial trading for its own sake has none. Dollar bills started out as convertible proxies for silver and gold, but now stand for nothing but themselves, again, pixels on a screen. When people want out of financial instruments and want to convert them to “real” things like shelter, food, and water, then the financial instruments disappear, along with a big chunk of our “economy”.
