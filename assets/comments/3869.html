There’s a difference between handling and road feel. The higher end versions of Mustangs, Camaros, Cadilacs and SRT’s put up track numbers comparable to their relevant competition. America is not a compact country with lots of narrow, winding roads. As a result, cars built for those streets are not going to be as popular as those built for long straightaways. Then there is the whole road feel side of things. A proper British sports car feels like a go-cart. It’s low, light, springy and great for darting around country roads with the top down. A proper Italian sports car is great to look at while you wait for the tow truck. The proper American sports car has the low end acceleration and torque that causes your head to slam into the headrest.My own tastes alternate between the British and American tastes.
